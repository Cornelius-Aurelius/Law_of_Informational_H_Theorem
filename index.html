Informational H-Theorem (Entropy Law)

Cornelius Aurelius — The Omniscientrix

Law Summary

The Informational H-Theorem states that informational entropy (measured via KL divergence or equivalent functionals) increases monotonically under irreversible dynamics, defining the informational arrow of time.

Simple Explanation

Disorder increases. Information spreads out. Systems naturally move toward balance.

The Informational H-Theorem captures this unavoidable push toward equilibrium — the informational equivalent of the Second Law of Thermodynamics.

Scientific Explanation

Let \( p(x,t) \) be an informational distribution and \( q(x) \) the equilibrium (often uniform). The informational entropy functional:

\( H[p] = D_{KL}(p \parallel q) = \sum_x p(x)\ln\frac{p(x)}{q(x)} \)

evolves monotonically toward 0 under dissipative or irreversible flow:

\( \frac{d}{dt} H[p(t)] \le 0 \)

This defines the informational arrow of time.

Variational Origin

Starting from δJ equilibrium and informational diffusion dynamics:

\( \partial_t p = -\nabla \cdot (p\nabla \ln p) \)

substituting into the KL functional yields:

\( \frac{d}{dt} D_{KL}(p \parallel q) = -\int p \|\nabla \ln \frac{p}{q}\|^2 dx \le 0 \)

thus proving monotonic entropy increase.
